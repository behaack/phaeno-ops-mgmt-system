# Getting started

Phaeno Portal keeps your organization’s laboratory requests, sample progress, released results, commercial documents, curated data, and user access in one place.

## Select the right organization

If your account belongs to more than one organization, use the organization switcher before starting work. The selected organization controls which records and actions you can see. Customer information never carries across organizations.

## Know your access level

Customer organization administrators can create and submit laboratory service requests, accept quotes, request cancellations, and manage organization members. Other active members can review their organization’s jobs, follow progress, and download results that have been released.

If an expected action is missing, first confirm that you selected the correct organization. Your organization administrator can then check your membership.

## Find your work

- **Dashboard** provides a starting point for the selected organization.
- **Lab services** contains laboratory requests, quotes, sample progress, and results.
- **Data Library** contains Phaeno-curated data packages explicitly assigned to your organization.
- **User management** shows the planned Customer-administration experience. It currently keeps changes only in session mock state; contact Phaeno for durable invitation or membership changes until the connected administration screen is released.
- **Documentation** opens these guides.

## Keep submissions safe

The initial laboratory request workflow is not a protected-health-information intake workflow. Do not enter patient names, dates of birth, medical record numbers, or other direct identifiers in request fields, notes, file names, or uploads.

Use your organization’s own reference values and follow the instructions attached to the selected analysis.

## Get help

When contacting Phaeno, provide the portal request or sample reference and a description of the step you were performing. Do not send credentials, authentication tokens, or sensitive sample content in a support message.
