# Statuses and recovery

Treat operational work, QuickBooks synchronization, notifications, file scanning, and file release as separate lifecycles. Recover the failed lifecycle without rewriting accepted scope or creating duplicate external documents.

## Triage order operations

Use Customer lab, Partner reagent, and Partner assembly queues to find work by its current status. Prioritize:

1. scan or safety failures that block intake or release;
2. payment or release-state conflicts that affect a ready result;
3. commercial records marked **Failed** or **Needs attention**;
4. Customer- or Partner-visible holds and requested corrections;
5. notifications that remain pending or failed.

The overall order status is not sufficient evidence. Check sample or shipment progress, the current quote, commercial document, scan state, release state, cancellation request, and notification record separately.

## Recover QuickBooks synchronization

Integration records progress through **Pending**, **Processing**, **Succeeded**, **Failed**, or **Needs attention**.

1. Open the authorized operational record and identify the failed operation.
2. Confirm the portal record still represents the intended business action.
3. Correct runtime connectivity, item mapping, customer mapping, or other configuration without changing the immutable accepted snapshot.
4. Retry the existing durable integration command.
5. Confirm a single QuickBooks document and the expected portal state before closing the item.

Do not create a replacement order or manually duplicate an estimate or invoice as a retry strategy. Idempotency protects the normal retry path; bypassing it can create conflicting commercial records.

## Recover files and releases

Files progress through **Pending**, **Scanning**, **Clean**, **Rejected**, **Failed**, or **Unavailable** scan states. Only clean files can continue to a valid release state.

- Investigate a rejected scan as a safety event; do not mark it clean manually.
- Retry a failed or unavailable scan only after the scanner or storage dependency is healthy.
- Keep output **Internal** while review is incomplete.
- Use **Ready** when operational review is complete, **Payment hold** when commercial release is blocked, and **Released** only when every applicable gate passes.
- Use **Withdrawn** when a previously available file must no longer be delivered; preserve the record and audit history.

Credit approval permits Net 30 release for the applicable Customer lab or Partner assembly workflow. Without credit, QuickBooks payment confirmation is required. Do not use an order-status change to bypass this rule.

## Recover notifications

Notification records use **Pending**, **Sending**, **Sent**, and **Failed**. Correct the delivery configuration or address, then retry the existing durable message. Confirm the final delivery state and avoid sending a separate untracked message as though it were the portal notification.

## Respect terminal and superseded states

Do not retry business actions that are rejected, declined, cancelled, withdrawn, or based on an expired or superseded quote. Start the documented replacement workflow when new scope, a new quote, or a corrected input revision is required.

For escalation, record the portal identifiers, lifecycle and visible status, safe error category, timestamps, retry count, and whether the external dependency is healthy. Keep credentials, webhook secrets, access tokens, Customer-confidential data, protected health information, and raw scientific files out of tickets and browser-bundled documentation.
