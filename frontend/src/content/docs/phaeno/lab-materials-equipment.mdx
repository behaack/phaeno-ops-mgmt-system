# Materials and equipment

Materials and equipment records show what was used, in what quantity, and whether it was suitable at the time of execution. They support scientific traceability; they are not purchasing or accounts-payable records.

## Receive and qualify material lots

Choose the lot kind first:

- **Supplier lot** uses a controlled material identity, supplier, supplier lot number, and storage location.
- **Prepared reagent** uses a controlled material identity, Phaeno lot number, storage location, and one or more source component lots. It does not use a supplier.

Select existing controlled records when available. If a material, supplier, or storage location is new, choose the corresponding **Create a new…** option and enter its name in the focused modal. The entered name returns as the selected option in the lot form. The new reference is created with the lot, and POMS assigns an immutable key to a new material. Retired reference records are retained for history but are not available for new lots.

Record the available quantity and unit. Use **Expiration or retest date** when applicable; this is a date-only field and the lot remains valid through the end of that date. Exact time-sensitive use-by control is not represented by this field.

New lots show **QC: Pending**. A laboratory supervisor uses **Record QC**, confirms the QC date, and selects **Pass QC** or **Fail QC** in the modal. A failed outcome requires a reason. Passing QC makes the lot eligible for controlled work when its expiration or retest date, availability, and other rules are also satisfied. Failing QC keeps the lot blocked. POMS also retains the operator and server-recorded audit time.

Only lots with **Passed** or **Approved exception** QC may be consumed in controlled work.

An approved exception must retain the decision, reason, approver, and applicable scope. Expired, failed, quarantined, or otherwise unavailable lots remain blocked.

When a prepared reagent is created, select each exact source lot and quantity. Every source lot must be QC-approved, unexpired, and available in the entered unit. POMS records the lineage and subtracts the component quantities atomically.

## Record consumption

Start the assigned execution before recording consumption. Select the exact lot, quantity, and compatible unit required by the protocol. The portal blocks unit mismatches and consumption above the available quantity.

Consumption is a durable scientific fact. Do not reduce or delete it to make inventory appear correct. Use an approved correction, adjustment, or exception that preserves the original entry and reason.

## Register equipment

Enter the equipment name, then select an equipment type and location already used by the laboratory. Protocol equipment requirements also supply type choices, and active material-storage locations are available as location choices. If the required type or location is missing, choose **Create a new…**, enter its name in the focused modal, and use the returned selection.

POMS assigns the immutable asset code when the equipment is created. Record the **Last calibration date** and **Calibration due date** when applicable. These are date-only laboratory facts; a time of day is not required. The due date cannot be before the last calibration date, and a last calibration date cannot be in the future. Equipment must be active and calibration-current at the time it is used.

## Record equipment use

Link the exact equipment record to the execution step or run. Confirm that its status and calibration cover the use time. If equipment becomes unavailable or overdue, stop new use, record the issue, and select another qualified item or follow the approved exception path.

QuickBooks may own a purchase-order reference, but supplier invoices, payment, and procurement approval remain outside Lab Ops.
