# Laboratory operations

Use **Lab operations** after a Customer accepts a laboratory quote. Quote acceptance records the Commercial authorization and creates the linked Lab work order together; do not create Lab work manually to repair an order.

Use the far-left sidebar beneath the toolbar to move among **Work**, **Protocols**, **Materials**, **Equipment**, **Batches**, and **Access**. The sidebar header identifies Lab operations and, on wider screens, provides the pin or unpin icon. Pointer users can preview an unpinned sidebar by approaching the left edge; keyboard and touch users open it from the edge tab. Choosing a section closes an unpinned sidebar.

## Understand the boundary

Lab Ops owns physical receipt, accession, container lineage, controlled protocol execution, material and equipment use, libraries, cross-order batches, sequencing sendouts, exceptions, and scientific approval. Commercial owns the Customer order, quote, cancellation review, billing, files, and publication.

Work enters Lab Ops only through an immutable Commercial authorization. Lab Ops publishes safe milestones and action requests back to Commercial; it never exposes internal laboratory tables or investigation notes to a Customer.

## Use the correct role

Laboratory roles are additive Phaeno-only assignments:

- **Lab Operator** records receipt, accession, containers, execution, consumption, equipment use, libraries, batching, sendouts, custody, and exceptions.
- **Lab Supervisor** can perform operator work, approve material QC, and resolve exceptions.
- **Protocol Administrator** authors, approves, activates, and retires controlled protocol versions.
- **Scientific Reviewer** records scientific approval after execution and blocking-exception gates pass.
- **Lab Operations Administrator** can perform all Lab actions and assign Lab roles.

Platform administrators retain bootstrap access. Only active Phaeno members can receive or use Lab roles; disabling or offboarding a user removes effective Lab access even if an assignment record remains. Assign the narrowest roles needed for a person's work. Separation of operator and reviewer is supported but is not mandatory for every service.

## Follow the work sequence

1. Open the authorized work order and verify the expected specimens.
2. Record receipt and accession; POMS assigns the authoritative barcode and creates the initial physical container.
3. Apply the intake decision and preserve every derived-container relationship.
4. Assign an active protocol version and record controlled execution.
5. Capture material consumption, equipment use, library preparation, batching, and sendout custody as applicable.
6. Resolve blocking exceptions and complete scientific review.
7. Publish **Ready for release** back to Commercial.

Every transition preserves prior state. Do not repair mistakes by deleting containers, overwriting protocol versions, replacing shipment history, or duplicating work orders.

## Operational readiness boundary

POMS now allocates checksummed Phaeno barcodes, renders Code 39 container labels, records reasoned print and failure history, supports scan-first container lookup, and adds eligible libraries to a draft batch by scan. These software controls do not prove that a particular printer, stock, scanner, browser, or environment is fit for production.

This guide explains the portal workflow; it is not a laboratory SOP or evidence that a production process has been validated. Before production use, Phaeno must approve the actual minimum fields, labels, printers, scanners, reprint and degraded-mode procedures, external NGS provider identifiers and manifest/status exchange, and operational ownership. Use only approved procedures and configuration for real work.
