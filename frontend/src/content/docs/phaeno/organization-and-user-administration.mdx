# Organization and user administration

Phaeno administrators manage organizations, Portal readiness, service entitlements, integration requests, and global access. Prospect, Customer, and Partner administrators manage membership only within their own organization. Access is invitation-based and every durable administrative change is audited.

## Know which administration surface owns each record

The **Accounts** directory and each account workspace are connected to the durable API. The directory contains Prospect, Customer, and Partner accounts; it does not contain the internal Phaeno organization. Changes to account details, Portal requests, service entitlements, members, and invitations made in those workspaces persist and are audited.

**User management** is also connected to durable account APIs. In the Phaeno context, active accounts and invited people appear in one list alongside global account status and every enforced Phaeno role. A **Pending invitation** status means the recipient has not completed account registration. Open a card’s actions menu to edit or deactivate an active user, reactivate a disabled user, or resend or revoke a pending invitation. Edit one active user to update their name, Platform administrator assignment, and additive laboratory roles together. Platform administrators retain bootstrap access to every laboratory capability even when no explicit laboratory role is assigned.

The role picker contains only roles enforced by the current authorization model: **Platform administrator**, **Lab operator**, **Lab supervisor**, **Protocol administrator**, **Scientific reviewer**, and **Lab operations administrator**. Authentication-provider metadata and unsupported job-title labels do not grant Portal access.

Cards marked **Pending invitation** still represent invitation records, not accepted user records. Enter the person’s first name, last name, and exact sign-in email, then select the Platform administrator and additive laboratory roles they should receive. The pending role intent grants no access. It becomes active only when the recipient accepts and becomes an active Phaeno member.

## Review and maintain accounts

Create the organization with the current business relationship:

- **Prospect** for an external organization that may receive explicitly granted curated data but cannot order work.
- **Customer** for organizations that request laboratory services.
- **Partner** for organizations that submit specimens for PSeq Lab Service or purchase the PSeq Kit, which includes data assembly.
- **Phaeno** for internal platform operations.

Do not use organization kind or Portal readiness as a shortcut for access. Curated package access requires an exact-version grant, member access requires an accepted invitation, and commercial work requires a current service entitlement.

Open **Accounts** to search the durable external-account directory. Select the account name to open its dedicated workspace, or use a row's **Edit** action for bounded maintenance of an existing Portal account. Normal onboarding begins in HubSpot, so the standard Accounts list and detail pages do not expose direct account creation or manual request submission.

When the Portal frontend and API are both running in their local development environments, the **HubSpot account intake** card provides **Simulate HubSpot account**. Enter the HubSpot Company name, requested relationship and services, Company and Deal identifiers, requested outcome, and optional internal notes. The simulator creates a durable `HubSpot`-sourced onboarding or Prospect-evaluation request in the account review queue. It never contacts HubSpot and does not create or activate an account, invite a user, grant a service, or mark the request applied by itself.

After approving a new-account request, select **Create account** and confirm the bounded creation action. POMS creates the durable Prospect, Customer, or Partner with **Pending** Portal readiness, associates the request, and opens the account details page. The request remains **Approved** until Phaeno completes and verifies the user, service, or other requested setup and records it with **Mark applied**.

Record readiness as **Not reviewed**, **Pending**, **Ready**, or **Blocked**, with an internal note when useful. Readiness describes operational setup; it does not invite a user, activate a service, or place an order.

## Review Portal requests

Use the Portal request queue to review onboarding, evaluation, service, relationship, Sales-assisted order, and offboarding handoffs. Live HubSpot-originated request intake is not connected in the current portal. The development simulator exercises the durable inbound account-request boundary without becoming a production manual-intake path. Existing durable requests remain reviewable. A future migration or recovery exception must use a separately restricted and audited procedure rather than ordinary account administration.

Approve or decline a pending request with a reason. Approval records the decision only. For an approved onboarding or evaluation request that has no account, use **Create account**; for other pre-organization recovery cases, select the completed organization while marking the request applied. Complete each approved invitation, entitlement, or order change through its owning workflow, verify the outcome, and then use **Mark applied** to record what was completed. This separation prevents a review click or account-creation click from silently granting access or creating commercial work.

Never put specimen, scientific, downstream-customer, credential, or other sensitive operational details in a HubSpot reference or request summary.

## Invite administrators and members

From the account details page, use **Manage users** or open the **Users** tab. Enter the HubSpot-designated contact’s exact sign-in email after Phaeno review, and assign **Administrator** only when the person must authorize commercial work or manage membership; otherwise assign **Member**. A HubSpot contact does not grant Portal access. Ask the recipient to sign in with the invited email and accept the Portal invitation.

Do not create a normal membership directly. Invitation acceptance provides the user-facing consent point and the audit link between invitation, user, and membership.

For an internal employee, select the Phaeno organization and open **User management**. Use **Add Phaeno user**, enter the person’s first name, last name, and exact sign-in email, and select the narrowest roles needed for their work. Grant **Platform administrator** only when platform-wide administration is required. The card remains marked **Pending invitation** until acceptance; afterward, open its actions menu and choose **Edit** whenever the profile or roles must change.

To follow up before acceptance, find the card marked **Pending invitation**, open its actions menu, and choose **Resend invitation**. Resending rotates the private link and is subject to a cooldown. Revoke an invitation that should not be accepted. Never copy invitation tokens into tickets, documentation, or logs.

## Convert a Prospect

Convert a Prospect to Customer or Partner only after the business relationship is approved. Conversion preserves the organization identifier, users, memberships, grants, and audit history. It does not create an order, assign broad data access, or configure prices automatically.

After conversion, complete the applicable operational setup: analysis availability and Customer credit for a Customer, or offerings, negotiated reagent pricing, assembly profiles, and Partner assembly credit for a Partner.

## Manage service entitlements

Use the organization’s **Services** tab to record dated commercial permission:

- Customers may receive **PSeq Lab Service**.
- Partners may receive **PSeq Lab Service** and **PSeq Kit**.
- Prospects and Phaeno organizations do not receive commercial service entitlements.

Set configuration to **Pending**, **Ready**, or **Blocked**. A service is usable only when its entitlement is effective and configuration is Ready. Overlapping periods for the same service are rejected.

When the entitlement implements an approved Portal request, select that request as its source. The selector shows only approved or applied requests for this organization that include the selected service. Leave the source blank only for a documented manual exception; do not attach an onboarding-only request to a service entitlement.

Use **End now** instead of deleting an entitlement. Review the consequence dialog and record why the entitlement is ending. The Portal retains and displays that reason with the ended entitlement.

PSeq Kit includes kit fulfillment followed by data submission and assembly. Do not create a separate data-assembly entitlement or sell data assembly without the kit.

## Deactivate and restore access

Deactivate a membership in the connected organization workspace to remove one organization relationship. Deactivate an organization when none of its memberships should be usable. Each action opens a consequence dialog; verify the named organization or person before confirming.

A deactivated membership is restored through a fresh invitation and acceptance. When all Portal access must stop, select **Deactivate** on the Phaeno user and confirm the named person. The account, memberships, assigned roles, and audit history remain, but the user immediately loses effective access. Use **Reactivate** to restore an authorized disabled account; then review its memberships and roles before considering access complete. Preserve historical records; do not delete users, memberships, or organizations to resolve access problems.

## Triage access safely

Confirm the person’s email, session state, selected organization, active membership, role, and organization status. Use record identifiers and audit events to trace changes. Do not ask for passwords, sign-in codes, invitation tokens, browser storage, or another tenant’s records.

If two administrators update the same record, the portal can reject the stale action. Reload the latest version, review the intervening change, and then make a deliberate new update rather than overwriting it.
