# Exceptions, rework, and cancellation

Exceptions preserve unexpected scientific or operational facts without exposing internal investigation detail to a Customer. Rework and cancellation decisions must retain the original work and its history.

## Classify the exception

Choose **Internal** when the issue can be resolved without Customer action. Choose **Customer action required** only when Commercial should present a safe request to the Customer.

Record the internal description, severity, blocking state, responsible role, and required next action. For Customer-action exceptions, add a separate safe summary that states what is needed without disclosing internal scientific hypotheses, other organizations, or confidential operational detail.

## Resolve or rework

A blocking open exception prevents scientific approval. A Lab Supervisor records the durable resolution, decision, and any authorized rework. Rework remains under the same Commercial order unless the approved Commercial process creates a new authorization.

Do not delete a failed execution, consumed material, rejected library, or prior QC result. Link the rework to the original facts and record why it was required.

## Handle cancellation requests

The Customer request remains in Commercial review. For unreceived work, Lab Ops can report that cancellation is safe. Started work requires a bounded work-order or specimen-level decision based on receipt, consumption, execution, and sendout state.

Do not force Lab work to cancelled merely because Commercial received a request. Return the Lab decision so Order Ops can approve, partially approve, or decline the Commercial cancellation and synchronize any financial adjustment.

## Recover projections

Commercial receives only safe action counts, summaries, milestones, schedule health, and timing projections. If a projection is delayed, refresh and retry the durable event. Never copy internal exception data into Commercial tables or create a duplicate work order.
