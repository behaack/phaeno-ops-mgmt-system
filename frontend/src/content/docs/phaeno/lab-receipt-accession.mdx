# Receipt and accession

Receipt and accession connect the Customer’s submitted specimen identity to the physical material handled in the laboratory. Record these steps in sequence and never create an unlinked laboratory specimen.

## Record receipt

Open the authorized Lab work order and confirm the expected submitted specimen, service, and Customer-safe identifiers. Record when and where the physical item arrived and any intake observation permitted by the approved procedure.

Receipt does not accept the specimen for processing. It establishes custody and allows accession to begin. If the physical item cannot be matched safely to the expected specimen, stop and record an exception rather than guessing.

## Accession the specimen

Accession only after receipt. Choose a unique accession number, then record location, quantity and unit when tracked, and an optional retention date. POMS creates the initial submitted-specimen container and assigns its authoritative Phaeno barcode automatically. Customer and Partner label values may be retained as references, but never replace the Phaeno barcode.

The label window renders the current container identity as a Code 39 barcode through the browser and installed printer driver. Enter the reason, open the system print dialog, and inspect the physical label before choosing **Label printed**. POMS increments the print count only after that confirmation. If the label did not print correctly, enter the failure details and choose **Record failed attempt**; the failure remains in history without falsely counting a usable label.

Every reprint requires a reason and renders the same current container identity. A reprint or failed attempt adds a separate history event; it does not replace the barcode or erase an earlier print. Verify readable text, barcode, accession, container, and adhesion using the approved bench procedure before processing continues.

## Scan and locate a container

Use **Work** > **Scan a container** to find the exact work order, accession, container status, location, parent barcode, and associated library. Place the cursor in the Phaeno barcode field and scan, or enter the complete barcode manually. Code 39 start and stop characters and letter case are normalized; an altered checksum, incomplete barcode, or unknown barcode is rejected without opening another record. The field returns focus after the result so repeated scans remain efficient.

## Preserve physical lineage

Create child containers for aliquots, prepared reagents, libraries, or other derived materials. Select the immediate parent container and record the quantity and unit required by the approved procedure. POMS assigns each child container its own barcode and opens its label window after creation. The lineage must allow an operator to scan a derived container and trace it through its immediate parent to the submitted specimen.

Do not reuse a barcode, move material by editing its parent, or delete a container to repair history. Record the supported correction or new derived container.

## Apply the intake decision

After accession, record **Accepted**, **On hold**, or **Rejected**. Holds and rejections require an approved reason code and a safe explanation when Customer action is required. When every expected specimen has an intake decision, the work order reaches **Received**.
